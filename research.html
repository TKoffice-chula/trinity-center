KMeD Clinical & Translational Research

Trinity's research engine bridges laboratory findings with patient care — through clinical trials, translational science, and global research networks that change practice guidelines.

Vision & Mission

KMeD Clinical Research

Vision: To set the national and global benchmark for the treatment and management of dialysis-related infections.

Mission: To conduct comprehensive epidemiological surveillance of peritonitis to reduce health loss and improve patient survival rates.

Translational Research Aims

  • Be a regional pioneer in applying advanced molecular diagnostics to nephrology and infectious diseases
  • Integrate high-throughput PCR techniques into standard diagnostic workflows
  • Solve the problem of culture-negative peritonitis
  • Develop protocols for detecting endotoxin-producing microorganisms
  • Enable Precision Medicine through targeted antimicrobial selection

Active Clinical Trials

A multi-arm research program advancing kidney care — from prevention to peritoneal dialysis.

Dialysis

STEP-PD Trial

Incremental vs. standard PD initiation — a pragmatic strategy to ease patients into peritoneal dialysis with fewer exchanges.

Prevention

National early-screening strategy — population-level screening and intervention to delay CKD progression at scale.

Changing Practice Guidelines

Trinity research has shifted clinical guidelines in four major areas of PD care.

Hypokalemia

Protocol-Based K Supplementation

First RCT-level evidence linking K correction to lower peritonitis risk. Target serum K 4–5 mEq/L — adopted into Thai PD standard of care and cited in updated ISPD guidelines.

Oral Health

Patient Self-Autonomy

OHIP-14 screening at PD entry — shifting prevention from clinic to patient. Poor oral health linked to 45% higher peritonitis risk and 55% higher all-cause mortality.

Culture-Negative

Standardised Sampling SOP

ISPD-aligned sampling protocol with central reference laboratory at Chulalongkorn — endorsed by NST as national PD-peritonitis sampling standard.

TB Peritonitis

Enriched-PCR Pathway

15-day enriched PCR: 87% sensitivity, cutting time-to-diagnosis from 32 to 17 days. Catheter retention now allowed if anti-TB started promptly — aligned with 2022 ISPD.
