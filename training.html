Interventional Nephrology Training

An intensive, two-week procedural training program at Trinity Center-ISN — combining hands-on procedural exposure, POCUS integration, and evidence-based clinical reasoning under expert supervision.

Program Overview

Two-Week Procedural Fellowship

Our fellowship is designed to achieve high levels of procedural competency for participants — observing or performing core nephrology procedures under professional supervision in a real-world clinical setting.

Trainees gain a thorough understanding of procedural indications, contraindications, and potential complications, while learning to integrate point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) directly into procedural decision-making.

Program Aims

  • Observe or perform core nephrology procedures under professional supervision
  • Understand indications, contraindications, and potential complications
  • Integrate POCUS directly into procedural decision-making
  • Manage peri-procedural risks (infections, bleeding, access complications)
  • Apply evidence-based practices in interventional nephrology

Core Procedures

The four foundational procedural competencies covered during the Trinity fellowship.

Vascular Access

HD Catheter Procedure

Duration: 3–4 days Setting: OR & bedside Modality: Tunnelled & Non-tunnelled

Comprehensive training in both temporary (non-tunnelled) and long-term (tunnelled) hemodialysis catheter insertion, with emphasis on ultrasound-guided technique, sterile protocol, and complication avoidance.

Skills Covered

  • Internal jugular & femoral vein access
  • Ultrasound-guided needle insertion
  • Seldinger technique & dilator passage
  • Subcutaneous tunnelling & cuff positioning
  • Catheter tip confirmation

Clinical Outcomes

  • Independent CVC insertion competency
  • Reduced procedural complications
  • Optimal catheter function & longevity
  • Management of malposition & thrombosis
Peritoneal Access

PD Catheter Insertion

Duration: 4–5 days Setting: OR · PD clinic Modality: Tenckhoff catheter (open & percutaneous)

Drawn from the experience of Thailand's largest PD Excellence Center, this module covers the full continuum of PD catheter care — from pre-procedure planning to long-term complication management — at the center that leads Thailand-PDOPPS and contributes to global ISPD guidelines.

Skills Covered

  • Tenckhoff catheter insertion technique
  • Exit-site & tunnel construction
  • Catheter break-in protocols
  • Exit-site & tunnel care
  • Catheter culture sampling technique

Complication Management

  • Bacterial & fungal peritonitis
  • Culture-negative peritonitis workup
  • TB peritonitis (enriched-PCR pathway)
  • Catheter dysfunction & migration
  • Exit-site infection prevention

Why Trinity for PD training: Our PD Excellence Center leads Thailand-PDOPPS (8-country global study), manages the MycoPDICS national surveillance network across 22 hospitals, and has produced ISPD-cited evidence that has changed practice guidelines worldwide.

Diagnostic

Kidney Biopsy

Duration: 2–3 days Setting: Inpatient ward · day-procedure unit Modality: Ultrasound-guided percutaneous

Hands-on training in ultrasound-guided percutaneous kidney biopsy — including pre-procedural risk assessment, real-time ultrasound localization, core sampling technique, and post-procedure monitoring for bleeding complications.

Skills Covered

  • Pre-biopsy risk stratification
  • Ultrasound localization of lower pole
  • Real-time needle visualization
  • Core needle technique (16G / 18G)
  • Specimen adequacy assessment

Clinical Outcomes

  • Adequate tissue sampling > 95%
  • Post-biopsy bleeding monitoring
  • Management of perinephric hematoma
  • Native & transplant biopsy approaches
Imaging

Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS)

Duration: 2–3 days · integrated Setting: Bedside & procedural Focus: Procedural & volume assessment

POCUS is integrated throughout the fellowship — not taught as a standalone skill but woven into every procedure. Trainees develop confidence in using ultrasound to guide decisions in real time at the bedside.

Core Applications

  • Vascular access (IJ, femoral, AV fistula)
  • Kidney biopsy localization
  • Bladder volume assessment
  • Hydronephrosis & obstruction
  • Lung B-lines for volume status

Advanced Applications

  • IVC for volume responsiveness
  • Cardiac function (basic views)
  • PD catheter position assessment
  • Fluid-status integrated decision-making
